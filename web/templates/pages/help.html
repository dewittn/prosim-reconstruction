How to Play PROSIM

Contents: Objective | Production | Weekly Decisions | Costs | Strategy Tips

Game Objective

You are the production manager of a manufacturing company. Your goal is to minimize total costs while meeting customer demand.

The game runs for a set number of weeks (typically 15). At the end, the company with the lowest total costs wins.

Production System

Production Flow

Raw Materials → Parts Department → Parts (X', Y', Z') → Assembly Department → Products (X, Y, Z)

Departments

Parts Department (Machines 1-4)

  • Converts raw materials into parts
  • Production rates: X' = 60/hr, Y' = 50/hr, Z' = 40/hr

Assembly Department (Machines 5-9)

  • Converts parts into finished products
  • Production rates: X = 40/hr, Y = 30/hr, Z = 20/hr
  • Each product requires 1 part (X→X', Y→Y', Z→Z')

Workforce

  • Untrained operators: 60-90% efficiency
  • Trained operators: 95-100% efficiency
  • Training costs $1,000 and takes 1 week

Quality

Approximately 17.8% of production is rejected due to quality issues. Higher quality budgets may reduce this rate.

Weekly Decisions

Budgets

  • Quality Budget: Invest in quality control (suggested: $500-1000)
  • Maintenance Budget: Invest in equipment maintenance (suggested: $500-1000)

Orders

  • Regular Raw Materials: 3-week lead time
  • Expedited Raw Materials: 1-week lead time (+$1,200)
  • Purchased Parts (X', Y', Z'): 1-week lead time

Machine Assignments

For each of the 9 machines, you decide:

  • Which part/product type to make (X/Y/Z)
  • How many hours to schedule (0-50)
  • Whether to send the operator for training

Cost Structure

Per-Product Costs

  1. Labor ($10/hour)
  2. Machine Set-Up (when changing part types)
  3. Machine Repair (random failures, $400)
  4. Raw Materials ($5/lb)
  5. Purchased Parts (varies by part type)
  6. Equipment Usage ($20/hour)
  7. Parts Carrying Cost (inventory holding)
  8. Products Carrying Cost (inventory holding)
  9. Demand Penalty (unfulfilled orders)

Overhead Costs

  1. Quality Planning (your budget)
  2. Plant Maintenance (your budget)
  3. Training Cost ($1,000/operator)
  4. Hiring Cost ($2,700/new hire)
  5. Layoff Cost ($200/week unscheduled, $400 termination)
  6. Raw Materials Carrying Cost
  7. Ordering Cost
  8. Fixed Expense ($1,500/week)

Strategy Tips

Early Game (Weeks 1-4)

  • Train your operators - efficiency gains pay off quickly
  • Order raw materials early (3-week lead time!)
  • Focus on one or two product types initially

Mid Game (Weeks 5-10)

  • Monitor demand forecasts and adjust production
  • Keep safety stock of parts for assembly
  • Balance production across product types based on demand

Late Game (Weeks 11+)

  • Reduce raw material orders as game winds down
  • Focus on meeting remaining demand
  • Minimize ending inventory (carrying costs)

General Tips

  • Shipping occurs every 4 weeks - plan ahead!
  • Unfulfilled demand carries heavy penalties
  • Consistent scheduling reduces setup costs
  • Quality and maintenance budgets prevent costly problems
